Threat and Error Management
Abbreviation TEM
Type Human Factors
Definition A safety procedure where an experienced pilot, instructor, or aviation safety expert observes a pilot to check whether necessary safety protocols have been followed or not.

Threat and error management (TEM) is a safety procedure where an experienced safety expert (pilot, instructor, safety consultant) monitors a pilot-in-command (PIC) and checks any behavioural anomalies that could go against safety protocols. TEM is one of the NOTECHS (non-technical skills) that aviation professionals should be aware of, because it provides useful qualitative or quantitative data on performance of pilots and air crew members.

As mentioned in aviation safety article, travelers (or customers) are very keen to know how safety is upheld at an airline. TEM is just "one building block" of overall aviation safety. Other "blocks" being NOTECHS, competencies, flight ratings, airworthiness etc. Threats are defined as any behaviour or machine malfunction that could immediately impact flight safety if neglected. There is a concept that most students have trouble understanding: Latent Threats. These are hidden threats, but it steadily impacts one aspect of flying. These latent threats must be found and fixed because they can lead to direct threats.
