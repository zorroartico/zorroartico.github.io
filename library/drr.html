Disaster Risk Reduction
Synonym DRR, natural disaster risk reduction
Coverage Global
Definition A subfield within geography that is about developing methods to minimize risks arising from natural hazards.

Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) is a narrow subfield within geography. If a meteorologist's work is to predict and provide a prognosis (forecast) for the weather in 2 hours, then DRR specialist's job is to monitor natural disasters and provide solutions to minimize impacts. However, specializations within DRR are broad. It could be about agricultural loss, plant disease, tornado, flood, climate-related technologies etc.

An example of DRR strategy would be preparing hurricane preparened manuals at major airports that lie within the path of a hurricane. Hurricane preparedness manuals provide information such as who to contact, what facilities to close down during emergency, and where to evacuate etc. These manuals must be constantly updated to reflect structural changes happening at an airport. The use of social media could be included as a DRR strategy, as well as amateur radio.
