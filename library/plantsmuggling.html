Plant Smuggling

This article belongs to the Biogeography category.

Plant Smuggling
Synonym Plant cyber crime, illegal plant trade, online plant trafficking [1]
Coverage Global
Cause Anonymity for illegal traders, low operating costs, lack of product inspection [1]
Effects Plant extinction, loss of natural habitat, desertification

Plant smuggling is exacerbated by the use of web, which allows looking up rare species of plants and their rarity. Among the species traded illegally, orchids share the largest pie [2]. Illegal logging is also part of the problem as well, as it leads to deforestation. Deforestation is a very serious problem because it causes loss of natural habitats for many wildlife species, including plants. It also blocks regulating CO2 and may cause soil erosion and less food production.

Cycads, a plant that is known to have existed since Jurassic era, are also being uprooted for illegal trade [3]. Due to its ancient history and its beauty as an exotic "palm tree-like" plant, it is becoming very popular. Its population is declining unfortunately. More attention is needed in uprooting 'plant blindness' - the tendency to ignore plants' importance in today's society.
